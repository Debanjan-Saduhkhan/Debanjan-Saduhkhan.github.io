Research Overview

My research in Machine Learning spans deep learning, statistical modeling, and optimization techniques. I specialize in developing interpretable ML solutions for complex business problems, including predictive analytics, recommendation systems, and energy optimization. My work combines traditional ML approaches with state-of-the-art techniques like TabNet and advanced neural architectures.

Key Publications

Estimating Surface Temperature from Thermal Imagery of Buildings for Accurate Thermal Transmittance (U-value)

Journal of Building Engineering 2020

This work presents a machine learning perspective on estimating surface temperatures from thermal imagery of buildings to accurately determine thermal transmittance (U-value). The research demonstrates the application of ML techniques in building physics and energy efficiency assessment.

Key Contributions:

  • Machine learning approach to surface temperature estimation
  • Thermal transmittance calculation from thermal imagery
  • Integration of ML with building physics
  • Accurate U-value determination for energy modeling

An Instance Segmentation and Clustering Model for Energy Audit Assessments in Built Environments

MDPI Sensors 2021

This paper introduces a multi-stage machine learning approach combining instance segmentation and clustering for energy audit assessments in built environments. The methodology provides a comprehensive framework for analyzing thermal imagery data using advanced ML techniques.

Key Contributions:

  • Multi-stage ML approach for energy audit assessment
  • Instance segmentation and clustering integration
  • Automated building performance evaluation
  • Advanced ML techniques in energy analysis
