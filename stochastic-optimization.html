Research Overview

My research in Stochastic Optimization focuses on multi-objective optimization problems in wireless sensor networks and distributed systems. I specialize in developing algorithms for lifetime-constrained networks, event-driven data gathering, and minimum-cost optimization under uncertainty. My work addresses fundamental challenges in resource allocation and network design under stochastic constraints.

Key Publications

Energy Efficient Multi-Beacon Guard Method for Periodic Data Gathering in Time Synchronized WSN

Wireless Networks, Springer 2020

This paper presents an energy-efficient multi-beacon guard method for periodic data gathering in time synchronized wireless sensor networks. The work focuses on optimizing energy consumption while maintaining reliable data collection in synchronized network environments.

Key Contributions:

  • Energy-efficient multi-beacon guard method
  • Periodic data gathering optimization
  • Time synchronized WSN protocols
  • Energy consumption reduction strategies
