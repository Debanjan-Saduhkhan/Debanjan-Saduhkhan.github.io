Research Overview

My research in Stochastic Optimization focuses on multi-objective optimization problems in wireless sensor networks and distributed systems. I specialize in developing algorithms for lifetime-constrained networks, event-driven data gathering, and minimum-cost optimization under uncertainty. My work addresses fundamental challenges in resource allocation and network design under stochastic constraints.

Key Publications

Minimum Cost Event-Driven WSN with Stochastic Lifetime Constraint

IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications 2019

This paper addresses the fundamental problem of minimum-cost event-driven wireless sensor network design under stochastic lifetime constraints. The work develops optimization algorithms that balance cost minimization with reliability requirements in uncertain environments.

Key Contributions:

  • Minimum-cost optimization under stochastic constraints
  • Event-driven WSN design with lifetime constraints
  • Stochastic optimization algorithms for network design
  • Cost-reliability trade-off analysis
