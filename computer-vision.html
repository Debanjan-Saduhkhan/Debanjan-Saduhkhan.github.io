Computer Vision

Object detection, Image processing, Thermal imaging

Research Overview

My research in Computer Vision focuses on thermal imaging applications for energy audit and smart building management. I specialize in object detection, instance segmentation, and thermal image processing techniques using state-of-the-art deep learning models like Mask R-CNN, Fast R-CNN, and Faster R-CNN for quantifying heat transfer coefficients and optimizing HVAC control systems.

Key Publications

Thermal Imagery Based Instance Segmentation for Energy Audit Applications in Buildings

IEEE Big Data 2019

This paper presents a novel approach to energy audit applications using thermal imagery and instance segmentation techniques. The work focuses on developing computer vision-based methods to quantify standard heat transfer coefficients (U-value) from thermal images of buildings.

Key Contributions:

  • Thermal imagery processing for building energy assessment
  • Instance segmentation techniques for energy audit applications
  • Quantification of heat transfer coefficients from thermal images
  • Real-world application in smart building management
